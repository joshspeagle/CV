Short Biography

Before beginning my Banting-Dunlap Postdoctoral Fellowship at the University of Toronto, I was a graduate student in the Department of Astronomy at Harvard University, where I completed my Master's and Doctoral Degrees from 2016-2020 under the joint supervision of Doug Finkbeiner, Charlie Conroy, Alyssa Goodman, and Daniel Eisenstein.

Before that, I was a researcher at the Kavli Institute for the Physics and Mathematics of the Universe (Kavli IPMU) from 2015-2016, where I worked with Alexie Leauthaud and Kevin Bundy on improving photometric redshifts for the Hyper Suprime-Cam Subaru Strategic Program (HSC SSP) Survey. This work was partly funded by a CREST grant from the Japan Science and Technology Agency (JST).

Before that, I was an undergraduate student at Harvard University from 2011-2015, where I completed my Bachelor's Degree with Honors in Astrophysics and Physics.

Outside of work, I mostly enjoy hanging out at home or going on walks with my wife Rebecca and our dog Kumo (pictured below).
